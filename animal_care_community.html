The Design Process

Understand, Research, Analyze, Ideate, Prototype, Test

Understand

I started by understanding and defining the problems people who work with animals face and what expert advice they look for. I conducted competitive analyses into Fiverr and Rover to gain insight into the market of expert apps and animal care apps.

Problem Statement

Professional animal caretakers need a way to access expert help that is timely, credible, specific, and cost-effective because they want to continue to improve and enhance the lives of the animals they are working with. We will know this to be true when we see an increased number of professional animal caretakers using this app to find the specific expert help they seek.

Hypotheses

Hypothesis 1: We believe that by building an onboarding page with a question(s) that reveals the user’s areas of interest we will achieve tailored expert suggestions to increase user satisfaction and positive feedback

Hypothesis 2: We believe that by creating a chat for users to communicate with experts, we will achieve an increase in users’ ability to get answers in a timely matter.

Research

I conducted interviews and surveys to gain an understanding of users’ needs and goals when using an expert app to I could focus my design on the real problems that potential users face.

Research goals

  1. Identify user pain points: What frustrations or challenges do users face when looking for animal care information?
  2. Understand user needs: What animal care information are users typically looking for when using applications or websites?
  3. Understand user behavior: How often would they seek expert help?

Insights

Community

  • Users want to feel safe, confident and empowered to ask questions to experts.
  • Users currently tend to reach out to their colleagues first to get questions answered.
  • Users want to see what other people are asking experts.

Resources

  • Access to many forms of communication is very important to users.
  • Users use many resources to find the answers they are looking for (e.g. colleagues, Google, forums, reputable organizations, peer-reviewed literature).

Reliable

  • Information on the internet can be hard to know if it is from a reputable source.
  • Getting information from credible sources is important to users. How long someone has been in a field does not make them an expert.
  • Users reach out to experts who are continuing to learn and adapt to new research and findings.

Interests

  • Users in animal care have a wide range of interests, but enrichment, animal care, and diet are the areas that users are most interested in.
  • Most users work with reptiles.

Timing

  • Getting questions answered within 1 - 3 days is ideal for most users. Users understand that their questions could take longer depending on an expert’s priorities.
  • Most users are looking for answers to their animal care questions weekly.

Situational

  • Who, where, and when a user will look for answers depends greatly on the situation.
  • Users will use different forms of communication with experts depending on the severity of the situation.

Accessibility

  • Accessibility is the factor that prevents most users from engaging with experts.
  • It would be beneficial for users to reach out to multiple experts in one place.

Usage

  • Most users would be likely to use an animal care expert app.
  • Most user that would use this app are between the age of 26 - 35.

User Testing

Utilizing my previous research, I developed a test plan and test script to use with real potential users as they tested my prototype.

The goal of the testing was to study the learnability, satisfaction, and identify any usability issues from first time users. We observed and measured how successful users were in navigating through the sign up/log in, messaging, and appointment features of the animal care community app.

Conclusion

Before this project, I had no previous design experience. From starting with sketches to submitting my last iteration of the Animal Care Community Expert App, I am extremely proud of all my work and all that I have learned. Through each iteration, I got to try something new and learn how to use more tools in Figma. As I worked on each new iteration, I always had the feedback and needs of users on my mind.

When I got to perform user testing, I was able to make the most adjustments to my design. This testing allowed me to connect more with potential users and gain more insight into the pain points that users experienced with earlier iterations of the app. For example, in the first high-fidelity iteration, I received feedback from users that they were unsettled by the subscription plans wireframe. They believed that the app would have free use, which it does, but it was confusing with how I originally set it up. This helped me learn that I need to be detailed, clear, and concise with each wireframe. Lastly, gaining a better understanding of material design was a great asset, I learned how to give a more seamless and transferable experience for users when interacting with my app.
